What is SAC?

The Student Advisory Committee (SAC) is a student group established within each academic unit to promote communication and interaction among students, faculty, staff, and administrators. SACs are both a social and academic organization within their academic unit, and as such, they advance involvement opportunities, encourage participation in departmental activities, and work together with others to build campus community.

FAQ

1. How Can I Join SAC?

Appointments to SAC are made by the Chairs of the Committee. Join the Student Advisory Committee and help build the School of Music community; submit an application for review. 

2. What Is the Role of the SAC Chairs?

The Student Advisory has two chairs, the Undergraduate and the Graduate Chair. Together, they are the leaders of the Student Advisory Committee. They have responsibilities to the School of Music and to the College of Fine Arts.  The responsibilities of each chair are outlined in the SAC Constitution . 

3. ​What Is the Role of the FAF Grants Representative?

The FAF Grants Representative is a member of the Student Advisory Committee. They are elected as the third FAF Grants Representative for the School of Music in addition to the two committee chairs. They also serve in other capacities as outlined by the SAC constitution , such as in RPT-SAC reviews. 

4. What Are SAC Departmental Offices?

SAC Departmental offices are offices of leadership within the Student Advisory Committee. The Student Advisory Committee is directed by the Undergraduate Chair and Graduate Chair, who may appoint Members of the Committee to serve in Departmental Offices such as Secretary or Social Media Coordinator. Each position has responsibilities that enable the committee to function efficiently. The roles and responsibilities for departmental offices are outlined in the SAC Constitution .
